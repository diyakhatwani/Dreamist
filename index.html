Find Your Perfect Fragrance

Choosing a perfume is about matching your scent with your personality, lifestyle, and mood. Below are short, practical tips arranged in two parallel columns so you can scan quickly.

Personality Match

Pick a scent that reflects who you are. Example: Floral for romantic souls, citrus for energetic ones.

Concentration Strength

Different concentrations last different times. Example: EDT for daytime, EDP for evenings.

Test on Skin

Always test a perfume on your skin — chemistry changes the notes. Example: Spray on your wrist, wait 5 minutes.

Less is More

Don’t over-spray; a few pulse-point sprays are enough. Example: Wrists, behind ears, collarbone.

Occasion Matters

Match scent to setting. Example: Fresh citrus for office, warm musk for evenings.

Storage Matters

Store in a cool, dark place away from heat and light to preserve notes. Example: Keep in the original box or cabinet.

Seasonal Choices

Pick scents that suit the season. Example: Light florals for summer, spicy amber for winter.

Layering Scents

Combine complementary notes for a signature fragrance. Example: Vanilla base with a floral top note.
